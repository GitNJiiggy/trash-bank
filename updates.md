# Trash Bank — Comprehensive Update

## Issues Identified & Fixes

### 1. Competitive Analysis — Other Cleanup Projects

| Project | What They Do | Limitation | Trash Bank Advantage |
|---------|--------------|------------|---------------------|
| **Plastic Bank** | Exchanges plastic for goods | Centralized, no governance | Decentralized DAO, work-based tokens |
| **ZeLoop** | App rewards plastic collection | No homeless inclusion | Vending machines for unbanked |
| **Plastiks** | Plastic credits on CELO | B2B focused | Consumer + DAO governance |
| **Empower** | Marketplace for plastic credits | Resell model | Direct redemption, no middleman |
| **PETCOIN** | Ghana plastic collection | Regional only | Global architecture |

**Key Differentiation:** Trash Bank combines:
- Work-based earning (like Plastic Bank)
- DAO governance (unique)
- Homeless inclusion (unique)
- Quadratic voting (unique)
- Soulbound reputation (unique)

### 2. Fair Launch — Addressing Hardcore Crypto

**Concern:** Zero supply start is unusual. Most projects need initial liquidity.

**Fix:**
- Initial liquidity pool: 5% of max supply for DEX bootstrapping
- Community treasury: 5% for development, marketing, partnerships
- Team allocation: 0% (no founder tokens)
- All remaining 90%: Earned through work only

**Comparison to Yearn (YFI):**
- Yearn: Zero pre-mine, liquidity mining only
- Trash Bank: Same model, but work instead of liquidity

**Comparison to Bitcoin:**
- Bitcoin: Mining from day 1
- Trash Bank: Work from day 1 (cleanup = mining)

**Response to skeptics:**
> "No different than Bitcoin mining — you contribute compute power, you earn BTC. You contribute cleanup work, you earn TTKN. The difference is cleaning communities instead of burning energy."

### 3. Homeless Framing — Reframe from Blame to Inclusion

**Before (problematic):**

**After (inclusive):**

**Use case positioning:**
- Primary: Community cleanup for all
