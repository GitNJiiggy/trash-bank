Trash Bank Whitepaper

Clean Communities, Built From the Ground Up

1. Executive Summary

Trash Bank is a community-owned protocol that rewards verified cleanup work with tradeable tokens and on-chain reputation. Unlike existing solutions, Trash Bank:

  • Is independent — not dependent on any single platform
  • Includes everyone — works for non-crypto users via vending machines
  • Is democratically governed — quadratic voting by contributors, not whales
  • Is fairly launched — zero team allocation, all tokens earned through work

2. The Problem

2.1 Global Waste Crisis

The world generates 2.1 billion tons of waste annually. Cities and municipalities bear the financial burden of cleanup, with the US alone spending over $11 billion annually on public space maintenance.

2.2 Excluded Populations

580,000+ individuals experience homelessness in the US (2024). Many want to contribute but lack economic pathways. Traditional apps require smartphones, bank accounts, and internet access—creating barriers to participation.

2.3 Existing Solutions Are Incomplete

Current cleanup platforms are:

  • Centralized — no community governance (Plastic Bank, ZeLoop)
  • Narrow in scope — plastic bottles only (ZeLoop, PETCOIN)
  • B2B focused — no consumer pathway (Plastiks, Empower)
  • Exclusive — require smartphones and crypto wallets

4. Tokenomics

4.1 Three-Tier Token System

Tier Name Type Purpose
1 Trash Coins Database (off-chain) Non-crypto users, vending redemption
2 Trash Tokens (TTKN) ERC-20 on Base Trading, staking, DEX liquidity
3 Garbage Cans Soulbound NFT Reputation, voting power, validator status

4.2 Why Soulbound Reputation?

In most DAOs, whales buy influence. Trash Bank prevents this: Garbage Cans cannot be bought or transferred. They can only be earned through verified cleanup work. Voting power is calculated as:

Voting Power = √(Garbage Cans)

This creates quadratic voting — a community member with 100 Garbage Cans gets 10 votes, while someone with 10,000 Garbage Cans gets 100 votes. Influence through contribution, not wealth.

4.3 Supply Distribution

Allocation Percentage Purpose
Liquidity Pool 5% DEX bootstrapping on Base
Community Treasury 5% Development, marketing, partnerships (governed by DAO)
Work-Earned 90% Distributed through validated cleanup tasks
Team/Founders 0% None. Founders earn tokens through work.

4.4 Why Work-Based Fair Launch Works

Trash Bank starts with 5% liquidity pool + 5% treasury. The remaining 90% must be earned through verified work. This is not "zero supply" — it's "zero free allocation." Everyone, including founders, must earn tokens through contribution.

4.5 Inflation Control

  • Annual cap: 10% maximum inflation
  • Dynamic adjustment: If cap is reached, per-task rewards decrease
  • Burn mechanism: Tokens burned on vending machine redemption

5. Proof of Work System

5.1 Task Flow

  1. Find Task — Browse cleanup tasks on McClaw or integrated platform
  2. Accept Task — Task escrow locks payment (MCLAW)
  3. Complete Work — Clean designated area
  4. Submit Proof — Upload 4 photos with GPS and timestamps:
    • Before: area condition
    • Collected: trash in bags
    • After: cleaned area
    • Disposal: trash in bin
  5. Validation — McClaw agent or high-karma community member approves
  6. Earn Rewards — Receive MCLAW + Trash Coins + Garbage Cans

5.2 Anti-Gaming Measures

  • GPS geofencing — Photos must be taken at task location
  • Timestamp verification — EXIF data must match submission time
  • Visual comparison — Before/after must show real change
  • Stake slashing — Validators lose reputation for approving fake submissions
  • Progressive trust — New users require multiple validations; high-karma users get auto-approval

6. Governance (DAO)

6.1 Trash Masters

Elected community members who distribute weekly budgets to sub-communities:

  • Election cycle: Every 2 weeks
  • Requirements: Minimum Garbage Cans threshold, active participation
  • Powers: Budget allocation, sub-community approval, proposal veto
  • Limitations: Cannot modify tokenomics, cannot mint tokens, cannot access treasury alone

6.2 Sub-Communities

Neighborhood or district-level groups that propose local cleanup priorities:

  • Formed by 10+ members with Garbage Cans
  • Propose budget allocations for their area
  • Vote on local priorities

6.3 Quadratic Voting

7. Homeless Inclusion

7.1 The Problem

Traditional crypto platforms require smartphones, bank accounts, and internet access. Homeless individuals are excluded from economic participation.

7.2 The Solution: Trash Bank Cards

  • NFC/QR Code Card — Like a transit card, tied to on-chain identity
  • Task Assignment — Via SMS, simple flip phone, or community liaison
  • Photo Submission — Community center kiosks or partner locations
  • Token Balance — Stored on-chain, accessed via card
  • Redemption — Vending machines for essentials (food, hygiene products)

7.3 Vending Machines

Trash Bank vending machines accept Trash Coins or TTKN:

  • Essentials: food, water, hygiene products
  • Partner locations: shelters, community centers
  • Geo-locked to prevent exploitation

8. Use Cases

8.1 Community Cleanup

Residents earn by cleaning their own neighborhoods. Municipalities lower costs while engaging citizens.

8.2 Event Cleanup

8.3 Corporate CSR

Companies sponsor cleanups for ESG reporting. Employees participate for team-building.

8.4 Homeless Economic Inclusion

Primary use case: vending machines for essentials. No smartphone or bank required.

10. Technical Architecture

10.1 Blockchain Layer

  • Chain: Base (Ethereum L2, low fees, fast finality)
  • TTKN Contract: ERC-20 on Base
  • Garbage Cans Contract: ERC-721 (soulbound, non-transferable)

10.2 Database Layer

  • Trash Coins: Off-chain database (PostgreSQL)
  • Task Records: Photos, GPS, timestamps
  • Reputation: Garbage Cans sync on-chain

10.3 Vending Machine Layer

  • NFC Reader: Reads Trash Bank Card
  • Balance Check: Queries database
  • Dispense: Burns tokens, releases product

11. Conclusion

Trash Bank creates economic pathways from waste:

  • For crypto-native users: TTKN on Base
  • For non-crypto users: Trash Coins in database
  • For everyone: Garbage Cans reputation
  • For governance: Quadratic voting by contributors

The protocol is standalone but enhanced by McClaw — any task platform can integrate. The more platforms, the more tasks, the more cleanup.

Clean communities, built from the ground up.
